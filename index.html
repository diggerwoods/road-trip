📜 The Quest Log 📜

CHAPTER 1 — JULY 29

⚔️ The Departure

Commerce City, CO → Burley, Idaho
Early morning departure! Cross the Rockies and head northwest through Wyoming into Idaho. Watch the landscape shift from plains to mountains to desert.
🏨 Camp: Holiday Inn Express, Burley
🎯 Side Quest: Count how many different state license plates you spot!
🚗 ~8 hours driving
😂 Joke of the Day: Why don't scientists trust atoms? Because they make up everything!
CHAPTER 2 — JULY 30

🏔️ Through the Snake River Plains

Burley, ID → Ellensburg, WA
Cross into Oregon and up through Washington. You'll pass through the dramatic Columbia River Gorge - a real-life version of a canyon level!
🏨 Camp: Holiday Inn Express, Ellensburg
🎯 Side Quest: Spot the wind turbines on the ridges
🚗 ~6.5 hours driving
📍 Nearby Adventures: Shoshone Falls (the "Niagara of the West" — 212 feet tall!), Snake River canyon views
😂 Joke of the Day: What do you call a sleeping dinosaur? A dino-snore!
CHAPTER 3 — JULY 31

🌊 Deception Falls & The Gathering

Ellensburg, WA → Lynden, WA
Drive over the Cascade Mountains through Snoqualmie Pass. Stop at Deception Falls — a thundering waterfall right off the highway! Then head north to Lynden for the wedding rehearsal dinner.
🏨 Camp: AirBnb in Lynden
🎯 Main Quest: Trevor's rehearsal dinner at 5:50 PM
⚡ Boss Encounter: Deception Falls — don't get splashed!
🚗 ~3.5 hours driving
📍 Nearby Adventures: Ginkgo Petrified Forest State Park, wild horses viewpoint near Vantage
😂 Joke of the Day: Why did the waterfall break up with the river? Because it was too clingy!
CHAPTER 4 — AUGUST 1

🫐 Berry Harvest & The Wedding

Lynden, WA (stay put)
Morning mission: Pick blueberries at Barbie's Berries! Then get fancy for cousin Trevor's wedding in Ferndale. Time to celebrate!
🏨 Camp: AirBnb in Lynden
🎯 Main Quest: Trevor's Wedding 💒
🫐 Side Quest: How many blueberries can you eat while picking?
🚗 No driving today!
📍 Nearby Adventures: Dutch village of Lynden (windmill!), Whatcom Falls Park (swimming hole + waterfall)
😂 Joke of the Day: What do you call a berry that's rude? A blueberry... wait, that's not right.
CHAPTER 5 — AUGUST 2

⚾ Cross the Border & Play Ball!

Lynden, WA → Vancouver, BC 🇨🇦
Cross the international border into Canada! Stop for a baseball game: Spokane Indians vs. Vancouver Canadians. New country, new adventure!
🏨 Camp: AirBnb in Vancouver
🎯 Main Quest: Watch the baseball game
🌟 Achievement Unlocked: Enter a new country!
🚗 ~1 hour + game time
😂 Joke of the Day: Why don't baseball players join unions? They don't like to be called out!
CHAPTERS 6-7 — AUGUST 3-4

🏙️ Vancouver Free Roam

Vancouver, BC (explore!)
Two free days to explore one of the coolest cities in North America! Possible adventures:
🎮 Science World — giant geodesic dome of science
🐋 Vancouver Aquarium — beluga whales and otters
🌲 Stanley Park — seawall bike ride
🦅 Capilano Suspension Bridge — 230 feet up in the trees
🍦 Ice cream on Granville Island
🎯 Side Quest: Try a food you've never had before!
🚗 Explore on foot!
📍 Must-Do List: Science World, Aquarium, Stanley Park seawall bike, Capilano Suspension Bridge, Granville Island market, Gastown steam clock (goes off every 15 min!)
😂 Day 6: What do you call a Canadian bear? A bear, eh?
😂 Day 7: Why did the French fry refuse to leave Canada? Because it was covered in gravy!
CHAPTER 8 — AUGUST 5

🛤️ The Trans-Canada Highway

Vancouver, BC → Radium Hot Springs, BC
Epic drive through the heart of British Columbia on the Trans-Canada Highway. Mountains, rivers, and wilderness everywhere. You'll pass through the Canadian Rockies — like a real-life Breath of the Wild map!
🏨 Camp: Radium Hot Springs (2 nights!)
🎯 Side Quest: Spot wildlife — bears, elk, and mountain goats live here!
🚗 ~8 hours driving
😂 Joke of the Day: What does a hot spring say when it's embarrassed? I'm steaming!
CHAPTER 9 — AUGUST 6

♨️ Hot Springs & Poutine Queen

Radium Hot Springs, BC (rest day!)
Soak in the natural Radium Hot Springs — warm mineral water surrounded by mountains. Then feast at the legendary Poutine Queen (fries + gravy + cheese curds = 🤤).
🏨 Camp: Same spot in Radium
🎯 Main Quest: Hot springs + poutine
♨️ Power Up: Hot springs restore all HP!
🚗 No driving today!
📍 Nearby Adventures: Radium Hot Springs pools, Sinclair Canyon hike (red rock walls!), Kootenay National Park trails
😂 Joke of the Day: Why do cows wear bells? Because their horns don't work!
CHAPTER 10 — AUGUST 7

🦬 Into Montana

Radium Hot Springs, BC → Billings, MT
Cross back into the USA and drive through Montana — "Big Sky Country." Wide open plains and huge skies. This is cowboy territory!
🏨 Camp: Holiday Inn Express East, Billings
🎯 Side Quest: Watch for pronghorn antelope — they're the fastest land animals in North America!
🚗 ~7 hours driving
📍 Nearby Adventures: Rimrocks (sandstone cliffs over the city), Pictograph Cave State Park (2000+ year old cave paintings!), Zoo Montana
😂 Joke of the Day: What did Montana say to Idaho? Nothing, states can't talk... or CAN they?
CHAPTER 11 — AUGUST 8

🏠 Return to Base

Billings, MT → Commerce City, CO
The final leg! Drive south through Wyoming and back into Colorado. You've completed the quest and can see the Front Range mountains welcoming you home!
🏠 Camp: HOME SWEET HOME
🎯 Achievement: Quest Complete! 🏆
🚗 ~9 hours driving
😂 Joke of the Day: Why was the car tired when it got home? Because it had a long trip!

🥔 Idaho — The Gem State

🥔Idaho grows about 13 BILLION pounds of potatoes every year — enough to fill 500,000 swimming pools!
🌙Craters of the Moon in Idaho looks so much like the moon's surface that NASA astronauts actually trained there before going to space!
💎Idaho is the only place in the US where you can find star garnets — a super rare gem with a star pattern inside.
🦅The Peregrine Falcon, which can dive at 240 mph (fastest animal on Earth!), nests in Idaho's Snake River Canyon.
🏔️Hell's Canyon in Idaho is deeper than the Grand Canyon — 7,993 feet deep!
🌊Shoshone Falls near Twin Falls is 212 feet tall — that's 45 feet TALLER than Niagara Falls!

🌲 Washington — The Evergreen State

🌋Mount Rainier is an active volcano and has more glaciers than any other mountain in the lower 48 states. It could erupt again!
🐙The Giant Pacific Octopus lives in Puget Sound — it can have an arm span of 20 feet and solve puzzles!
🍎Washington grows more apples than any other state — about 10-12 BILLION apples every year.
🌊Deception Falls (where you're stopping!) drops 60 feet through a narrow rock gorge carved by 10,000 years of water.
🎮Both Nintendo of America and Valve (who make Steam) have their headquarters in Washington!
🌲Washington's Olympic Rainforest gets 12-14 FEET of rain per year — trees there are draped in moss like a fantasy forest!
🐌The banana slug (bright yellow, up to 10 inches long!) lives in Washington's forests and is actually helpful — it recycles dead plants.

🇨🇦 British Columbia — The Pacific Province

🐻BC is home to the Spirit Bear (Kermode Bear) — a black bear born completely white! Only about 400 exist in the wild.
🌲Cathedral Grove in BC has trees over 800 years old — they were alive during the time of the real Knights Templar!
♨️Radium Hot Springs gets its name from the trace amounts of radon gas in the water (totally safe!) — the water is naturally heated to 40°C (104°F) by the Earth itself.
🎬Vancouver is called "Hollywood North" because SO many movies and TV shows are filmed there. Lots of Marvel movies were filmed in Vancouver!
🦑BC's coast has the Ogopogo — Canada's version of the Loch Ness Monster, living in Okanagan Lake. Sightings go back hundreds of years!
🍁Canada's $1 coin is called a "Loonie" because it has a loon (a bird) on it. The $2 coin? A "Toonie!"
🏔️BC has over 300 provincial parks — more than most entire countries have national parks!
⏰The Gastown Steam Clock in Vancouver is powered by steam and whistles every 15 minutes. It was built in 1977 to cover a steam vent!

🦬 Montana — Big Sky Country

🦕More T-Rex fossils have been found in Montana than any other state! The famous "Sue" was found nearby in South Dakota.
🐂Montana has 3x more cows than people. The human population is about 1.1 million, but there are over 2.5 million cattle!
⚡Montana gets some of the most intense lightning storms in the country because of its flat terrain meeting the Rocky Mountains.
🏔️Glacier National Park in Montana has over 700 miles of trails and 130 named lakes. Its glaciers have been there for 7,000 years (but they're shrinking).
🌡️Montana holds the record for the biggest temperature change in one day: 103°F drop in 24 hours (from 44°F to -56°F) in 1916!
🎨Pictograph Cave near Billings has cave paintings over 2,000 years old — ancient people drew animals and hunting scenes there!
🦬Before settlers came, 30-60 MILLION bison roamed Montana's plains. Now there are about 500,000 in all of North America.

🏔️ Wyoming — The Cowboy State (passing through!)

🦬Yellowstone has a supervolcano underneath it. If it erupted, it would cover most of the US in ash — but don't worry, it's not expected to blow for thousands of years!
🤠Wyoming has the smallest population of any US state — only about 580,000 people. Your school probably has more kids than some Wyoming towns!
🏜️Devils Tower in Wyoming was the first National Monument in the US and looks like a giant tree stump made of rock. It was featured in "Close Encounters of the Third Kind."
🦌Wyoming has more pronghorn antelope than people! They can run 55 mph and are the second fastest land animal on Earth.

🌎 Epic Road Trip Facts

🚗The longest road in the world is the Pan-American Highway at 19,000 miles — your trip is about 2,800 miles, so you'll drive about 15% of it!
🏔️The Rocky Mountains stretch 3,000 miles from New Mexico all the way to northern British Columbia — you'll cross them TWICE!
🌊The Columbia River Gorge (you'll drive through it!) is 80 miles long and up to 4,000 feet deep. It's the only sea-level route through the Cascades.
🗺️The US-Canada border is 5,525 miles long — the longest international border in the world between two countries!
